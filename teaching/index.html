Teaching Philosophy

I've been fortunate enough to be surrounded by people who value personal growth and continuous understanding of the world around us. Even more fortunate is that I have developed the same habits. Educating ourselves and others is how we deepen our experience of this world.

If willingness to learn are one of the branches that make up the tree of a person, then mindfulness, gratitude, hope and resilience make the trunk. If in the classroom exercises take place that promote these underlying values in students not only will they be more receptive to learning but take these skills throughout their adult life. What makes a valuable citizen in our society is mathematical and scientific literacy, yes, but also a population that has well-being as a focus also.

Inherently, children are curious and have a deep desire to learn, my role is to engage that curiosity and direct it towards the learning of mathematical problem solving and scientific enquiry. Although I will predominantly be in control of the content they are to learn relevant to the Australian Curriculum, they are to direct their own learning of said content. The 5E Instructional Modal is a useful tool for my explicit instruction, the structure of my unit plans and lesson plans and most importantly this pedagogy creates a medium in which mathematical and scientific enquiry is the norm. Creating a culture of curiosity and asking questions lays the foundation for my students in any path they wish to take in life.

In order for students to begin the learning process, they must be engaged in, students will engage when they see a need or reason for their use. I endeavour to give my students an authentic learning experience wherever possible in order to maximise their academic potential.

A challenge in the classroom is to acknowledge the differences in students' abilities. The 5E Instructional Model addresses this well using strategic questioning and cooperative learning to facilitate the students' knowledge acquisition. Part of this learning process involves Vygotski's Zone of Proximal Development and students teaching each other with myself acting as the facilitator of their questioning and discussion.
